Why Pajama Parties Can Change Europe

Night trains are the most climate-friendly and comfortable way to travel long distances in Europe. But the network that once connected our continent has largely disappeared due to political neglect.

What is a Pajama Party?

A pajama party is a peaceful, creative demonstration where people gather at train stations wearing pajamas and sleep accessories (eye masks, pillows, stuffed toys) to advocate for night trains. It's a "silent disco" format with synchronized music through headphones, making it media-friendly, legally safe, and inclusive for all ages and mobility levels.

Proven success: The October 2024 Trans-European Pajama Party brought together activists across multiple European cities, generating media coverage and demonstrating public demand for night trains.

That's where you come in! By organizing pajama parties at train stations, we create visible, media-friendly protests that show politicians exactly where people want night trains to go. Your participation helps generate the data and pressure needed for policy change.

This platform is part of Back-on-Track, a European association advocating for the return of night trains. We believe grassroots activism - starting with you - can rebuild the sustainable transport network Europe deserves.
